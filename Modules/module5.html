📌 What is VAT / GST?

VAT (Value Added Tax) and GST (Goods and Services Tax) are indirect taxes levied on the sale of goods and services. GST is a modern, unified version of VAT used in countries like India, while Kenya still uses VAT.

🎯 Importance of VAT/GST in Accounting:

  • Legal requirement for businesses over a certain turnover.
  • Helps in accurate tax reporting and compliance.
  • Allows input credit on purchases.
  • Improves transparency in transactions.

⚙️ How to Enable VAT / GST in Tally:

  1. Go to Gateway of Tally → F11: Features → Statutory & Taxation
  2. Set Enable Goods and Services Tax (GST) or Enable Value Added Tax (VAT) to Yes.
  3. Select your Country (e.g., Kenya) and State/Region.
  4. Choose the type of tax applicable (GST, VAT).
  5. Press Ctrl + A to save the settings.

📝 Company VAT Setup:

  • Enter company’s VAT Registration Number.
  • Set applicable VAT/GST rates (e.g., 16%, 0%).
  • Create VAT classes if needed for sales/purchases.

💼 Creating Tax Ledgers in Tally:

Go to Accounts Info → Ledgers → Create

  • Ledger Name: VAT 16% or GST Output
  • Under: Duties & Taxes
  • Type of Duty/Tax: VAT or GST
  • Percentage of Calculation: e.g., 16%

🧾 Recording VAT/GST Transactions:

  • Use Purchase Voucher (F9) or Sales Voucher (F8).
  • Select customer/supplier.
  • Select items and quantities.
  • Add VAT/GST ledger to calculate tax.
  • Save the voucher with Ctrl + A.

📑 Reports Related to VAT / GST in Tally:

  • VAT Computation / GST Summary
  • Tax Ledger Summary
  • Sales and Purchase Register
  • Input Tax Credit Summary

💡 Tips for Managing VAT/GST in Tally:

  • Verify tax ledgers and rates during setup.
  • Ensure correct classification of taxable and non-taxable items.
  • Reconcile tax reports before filing returns.
  • Backup data regularly to avoid loss.
