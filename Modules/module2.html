üìò Topic 1: What is a Ledger?

A ledger in Tally is a record that tracks individual accounts like Cash, Sales, Purchases, Capital, etc. All transactions are posted under ledgers.

How to Create a Ledger in Tally:

  1. From Gateway of Tally, go to Accounts Info ‚Üí Ledgers ‚Üí Create
  2. Enter the name of the ledger (e.g., Cash, Sales, Rent)
  3. Select the appropriate Group (e.g., Cash under Cash-in-Hand, Rent under Indirect Expenses)
  4. Enter Opening Balance (if applicable)
  5. Press Ctrl + A to save

üìó Topic 2: What is a Voucher?

A voucher is a document that records financial transactions. Tally has many types like:

  • Payment Voucher (F5)
  • Receipt Voucher (F6)
  • Sales Voucher (F8)
  • Purchase Voucher (F9)
  • Contra Voucher (F4)

How to Record a Voucher Entry:

  1. Go to Gateway of Tally ‚Üí Accounting Vouchers
  2. Select the type of voucher using function keys (F5‚ÄìF9)
  3. Enter the Date, Ledger, Amount and Narration
  4. Press Ctrl + A to save

Topic 3: Ledger and Voucher Creation & Entries (Double Entry Accounting)

This topic explains the accounting principles and the structure behind every transaction in Tally.

  • Double Entry System: Every transaction has two sides ‚Äî Debit and Credit ‚Äî which must always be equal. This system ensures accuracy and completeness in records.
  • Golden Rules of Accounting:
    • Personal Accounts: Debit the receiver, Credit the giver
    • Real Accounts: Debit what comes in, Credit what goes out
    • Nominal Accounts: Debit all expenses and losses, Credit all incomes and gains
  • Types of Accounts:
    • 1. Personal Accounts: Related to individuals, firms, companies (e.g., Mr. Ali A/C, XYZ Ltd.)
    • 2. Real Accounts: Tangible and intangible assets (e.g., Cash A/C, Furniture A/C, Patent A/C)
    • 3. Nominal Accounts: All expenses, losses, incomes, and gains (e.g., Rent A/C, Salary A/C, Interest Received A/C)
  • Ledger Groups: These are predefined categories in Tally such as Capital Account, Loans, Fixed Assets, Sales, Purchase, Direct Expenses, Indirect Income, etc.
  • Creating Ledgers: Go to Gateway of Tally > Accounts Info > Ledgers > Create. You must assign each ledger to the correct group.
  • Voucher Types:
    • Payment: For all cash/bank payments
    • Receipt: For all income/cash received
    • Contra: Cash deposited to/withdrawn from bank
    • Journal: Adjustment entries
    • Sales: Records sales of goods or services
    • Purchase: Records purchases of goods or services
  • Sample Double Entry Transactions:
    • Cash Sale of Goods: Dr. Cash A/C, Cr. Sales A/C
    • Purchase from Supplier: Dr. Purchase A/C, Cr. Supplier A/C
    • Salary Paid: Dr. Salary A/C, Cr. Cash/Bank A/C
    • Bank Deposit: Dr. Bank A/C, Cr. Cash A/C
    • Rent Received: Dr. Cash A/C, Cr. Rent Received A/C
  • Using Day Book: Gateway > Display > Day Book ‚Äì view, filter, or modify all vouchers entered on a specific day.
  • Trial Balance: Ensures that total debits equal total credits. It is the foundation of financial statements.
  • Debit/Credit Identification Tips:
    • Asset Increases ‚Üí Debit
    • Liability Increases ‚Üí Credit
    • Income/Gain ‚Üí Credit
    • Expense/Loss ‚Üí Debit
