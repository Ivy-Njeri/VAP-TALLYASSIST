🏦 Topic 1: Banking in Tally

Tally provides built-in banking features to help businesses manage their bank transactions, generate cheques, and perform bank reconciliation.

🔐 Key Banking Features in Tally:

  • Cheque Management – Issue, print, and track cheques.
  • Bank Allocations – Split transactions for deposits or payments.
  • Auto Bank Reconciliation – Match Tally entries with bank statements.
  • e-Payments (optional) – Supports integration with banks for digital payments (in advanced Tally versions).

💳 How to Create a Bank Ledger in Tally:

  1. Go to Accounts Info → Ledgers → Create
  2. Name the ledger (e.g., Equity Bank, KCB, Co-operative)
  3. Select the group as Bank Accounts
  4. Enter account number, IFSC, and other bank details
  5. Set opening balance if any
  6. Press Ctrl + A to save

📄 Topic 2: Bank Reconciliation Statement (BRS)

A Bank Reconciliation Statement (BRS) is a report that compares the bank balance in your books with the balance in your actual bank statement. It helps identify:

  • Unpresented cheques
  • Bank charges and interest not recorded
  • Customer deposits directly to bank
  • Errors in data entry

📝 Why BRS is Important:

  • Ensures your books match your actual bank balance
  • Helps detect errors, fraud, or missed entries
  • Supports accurate financial reporting

🔧 How to Perform BRS in Tally:

  1. Go to Gateway of Tally → Banking → Bank Reconciliation
  2. Select the bank ledger (e.g., KCB, Equity)
  3. The screen shows:
    • Book balance (Tally records)
    • Unreconciled transactions
  4. Enter the bank date (as per bank statement) next to each transaction
  5. Save the changes. Reconciled transactions will now match the bank.

📌 Tally Tips:

  • Regularly reconcile monthly to avoid errors
  • Use “Bank Date” to mark transactions instead of editing voucher date
  • Attach scanned bank statements for reference (in some advanced Tally setups)
